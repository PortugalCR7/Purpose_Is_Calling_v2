# Master creative brief (v1 — original, unedited)

> This is the client's original brief, preserved verbatim for reference.
> Do not edit this file casually — if the brief changes, bump a version
> note at the top rather than silently rewriting history. The *executable*
> version of this direction, with the rune-portal issue corrected, lives

## Project

Design and produce a premium cinematic hero animation for a
transformational coaching brand. The animation should feel like a
continuous first-person journey through an Icelandic volcanic landscape
that culminates in the revelation of an individual's Sacred Instructions.

The visual language should prioritize realism, atmosphere, scale,
restraint, and emotional anticipation over fantasy spectacle.

The pacing should closely emulate the supplied motion reference: slow,
deliberate, cinematic forward movement with smooth transitions and
minimal visual noise.

Think: Denis Villeneuve, Roger Deakins, National Geographic, Iceland,
Fire and Ice, Geological monumentality.

NOT: fantasy, magic portals, video game environments, excessive visual
effects, ornate mythology.

## Core visual language

Every scene should feel like it exists within one continuous geological
world. Reference real Icelandic locations and textures: basalt column
cliffs, obsidian lava fields, geothermal steam, volcanic ash, glacial
mountains, black sand, cooled lava, fissured rock, geothermal vents,
weather, atmospheric haze.

Avoid: glowing runes, decorative carvings, floating symbols, fantasy
architecture, impossible structures. If symbolism exists, it should
emerge naturally from the geology.

## Camera language

The camera is the protagonist. It never behaves like a drone. It never
spins. It never rushes. Movement should feel like someone being quietly
pulled toward something ancient. The reference motion establishes the
pacing.

Movement characteristics: continuous forward dolly, subtle camera
inertia, gentle parallax, no abrupt cuts, no whip pans, no dramatic
rotations, cinematic easing, restrained speed, immersive perspective.

The environment should remain largely still. Only one important thing
changes within each sequence.

## Workflow

This project should NOT be generated as one long video. Instead:

1. Design six photorealistic master stills. Each still functions as a
   keyframe.
2. Animate each still independently using the supplied motion reference.
3. Connect the six clips into one continuous journey.

This approach produces much greater visual consistency than asking a
model to invent the entire sequence at once.

## Sequence (original scene descriptions)

**Scene one — The call.** Purpose: establish destination, no magic, no
spectacle. A wide volcanic valley. Foreground: a clear path of cooled
lava or black sand. Midground: massive basalt cliffs naturally frame the
composition. Background: snow-covered mountains. Far in the distance,
embedded naturally into the canyon wall, sits a dormant circular
threshold carved from the same basalt. The threshold should be very
small. The landscape is the hero. Animation: camera slowly advances down
the path. Nothing else changes.

**Scene two — Recognition.** The place recognizes the traveler. Identical
environment. The dormant threshold begins showing extremely subtle
glowing fractures. Small dust particles fall. Heat shimmer becomes
visible. Nothing opens, no fire yet. Animation: continue identical
forward movement, only the threshold changes.

**Scene three — Threshold.** Stone becomes passage. The center of the
ancient stone threshold gradually transforms into a calm membrane of
molten amber light resembling liquid sunlight beneath translucent glass.
Surrounding basalt remains geological and weathered. Animation: forward
movement continues, the membrane gently ripples, near the end it slowly
separates, creating a narrow passage. Camera never stops.

**Scene four — Crossing.** Transition. First-person perspective entering
the threshold, passing through dense flowing amber light behaving like
liquid plasma rather than flames. Obsidian frame visible only at the
edges. Warm volumetric light surrounds the viewer, photoreal. Animation:
continue uninterrupted forward movement, no spinning, no vortex, no
explosive effects, light flows naturally around the camera.

**Scene five — The descent.** Movement into deeper consciousness. Not a
fantasy tunnel — a vast subterranean Icelandic volcanic chamber. Towering
basalt columns, obsidian caverns, geothermal steam, underground rivers,
cooled lava, shafts of light through cracks in the earth. Geological
formations naturally suggest archetypal human forms, broken masks, faces
within stone, weathered silhouettes, without becoming literal sculptures.
Quiet, ancient, immense. Animation: camera continues slowly forward and
slightly downward, steam drifts, light reveals formations, no floating
symbols, no decorative particles.

**Scene six — Revelation.** Arrival. A perfectly still circular chamber
carved from polished obsidian. At its center, a monumental polished
volcanic glass monolith — the Sacred Mirror. Surface begins completely
black and reflective. As the viewer approaches, luminous golden sacred
geometry and symbolic language gradually emerge beneath the glass surface
as though they have always existed within the stone. Chamber remains
quiet, minimal, monumental. Animation: forward motion slows almost to
stillness, symbols reveal beneath the glass, final frame holds long
enough for future personalization.

## Art direction rules (original)

Photorealistic first. Geological realism over fantasy. Iceland is the
primary visual reference. One continuous world. One camera. One
emotional journey. One transformation per scene. Environmental
storytelling over symbolic decoration. Monumental scale. Minimal visual
effects. Restrained pacing. Premium editorial cinematography.

## Motion reference

Use the supplied motion reference ONLY for pacing, camera movement,
acceleration, deceleration, cinematic rhythm, forward momentum. Do not
copy its environment, architecture, or colors. Only replicate its
movement language.

## Production note (original)

Keep the camera height constant through the first four scenes —
approximately eye level (1.6–1.8m). Only introduce the subtle downward
movement after crossing the threshold. That gives the viewer a tangible
spatial cue that they've entered a different realm without relying on
heavy visual effects, and it preserves the grounded, photoreal quality.

## Review note added at intake (v1.1)

The four reference stills supplied alongside this brief do not meet the
brief's own rules — they show large rune-inscribed stargate structures
with decorative ember particles and lava-lightning membranes, which is
close to a line-by-line match against this document's own "NOT" list.
They're retained as color/temperature references only (see
`art-direction.md` → grade recipe). The six production prompts in
`film/prompt-bible.md` rewrite every scene to honor this brief's original
intent — small threshold, no runes, no particles until scene four,
membrane as calm liquid light rather than lightning. Treat
`prompt-bible.md` as this brief corrected back to itself.
